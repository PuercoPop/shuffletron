Shuffletron

Shuffletron is a music player for Linux. It runs in the terminal, with a textual interface, and has support for search, labelling files using arbitrary tags, and a queue. It is designed to let me spend more time playing music and less time clicking around a GUI - the tagging features help me make note of interesting songs I've overlooked while shuffling through my whole library, and the search features allow me to quickly find specific songs I want to hear. It starts up quickly and copes well with large music libraries (tens of thousands of files). It is written in Common Lisp (but don't let that put you off). Compiling from source requires SBCL or a recent version of the Clozure CL compiler (SBCL is recommended). Compiling a binary follows the standard 'make' / 'make install' process (atypical for lisp programs) and uses the implementation's respective application delivery support to produce an executable. Clozure CL users may have to edit build-ccl.lisp to tweak the ASDF search path.
